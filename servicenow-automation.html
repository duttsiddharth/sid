// The Challenge

The Challenge: ServiceNow as a System of Record, Not a System of Intelligence

Most enterprises run ServiceNow as a well-governed system of record — and stop there. Licence lifecycle, RBAC, and SSO are usually under control, but the platform's potential as an automation and AI layer often goes untapped, partly because platform teams and AI initiatives sit in different parts of the organization.

At the same time, vendor sprawl is a real cost problem. Enterprises running multiple SaaS platforms alongside ServiceNow frequently carry redundant licensing and inconsistent governance models across 10+ tools, with no single owner accountable for rationalizing the portfolio.

The risk of getting automation wrong on a platform like ServiceNow is also asymmetric — a poorly governed integration or AI workflow can introduce compliance gaps far faster than it removes manual effort. That's why any automation work on ServiceNow has to be designed by someone who treats governance as a first-class requirement, not an afterthought to be cleaned up later.

// My Approach

My Approach: Governance First, Automation Second

I've managed enterprise SaaS platform portfolios including Avaya OneCloud and ServiceNow directly — overseeing licence lifecycle, RBAC governance, SSO integration, and API-based customizations for 15+ global enterprise clients with zero compliance breaches. That governance discipline comes first; automation and AI are layered on top of it, not instead of it.

On the automation side, I've integrated Claude API and n8n directly with ServiceNow to build Smart Desk, an AI-powered IT support triage system that classifies and routes tickets automatically through existing ServiceNow workflows — achieving ~42% ticket auto-resolution without circumventing the platform's existing governance model. I also drive SaaS vendor evaluation and contract negotiation, having rationalized a 10+ tool SaaS portfolio to eliminate redundancy and reduce annual licensing costs by approximately 12%.

Every API-based customization or workflow automation is documented and access-controlled the same way the rest of the platform is — so when an audit happens, the AI-assisted workflows hold up to the same scrutiny as everything else in your ServiceNow instance, rather than becoming the exception that compliance teams flag.

Security review is built into the process, not added afterward. Every integration goes through the same scrutiny as any other ServiceNow change — confirming exactly what data it touches, what access it requires, and what the rollback plan looks like if something needs to be reversed. That discipline draws directly on AWS Solutions Architect and ISC2 Cyber Security training: API integrations are evaluated for the attack surface and data exposure they introduce, not just for whether the automation works, which matters as much on a governed enterprise platform as it does on cloud infrastructure.
